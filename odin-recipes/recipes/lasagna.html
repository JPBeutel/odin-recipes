Vegan, Gluten Free Lasagna with Cashew Ricotta

Layers of gluten-free lasagna noodles, zucchini, spinach, and homemade cashew ricotta make this healthy veggie loaded, gluten-free, vegan lasagna.

this is a one dish meal, and a classic family friendly favorite you'll come back to week, after week

Ingredients

  • 32 oz jar of marinara sauce
  • 1 box of gluten-free lasagna sheets
  • 3 medium zucchini sliced thinly
  • 16 oz bag of spinach

Cashew Ricotta

  • 1 1/2 cup raw cashews
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1 tbsp nutritional yeast
  • 1 tbsp apple cider vinegar
  • 1 clove of garlic
  • 1/2 tsp onion powder
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1/4 tsp pepper

Vegan Bechamel Sauce

  • 3 tsp vegan butter
  • 2 tsp gluten-free flour
  • 2 cups soy milk
  • 1/4 tsp nutmeg
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1/4 tsp pepper

Instructions

Cashew Ricotta

  1. Boil cashews in hot water for 10 minutes. Drain, and rinse.
  2. In a food processor combine all ingredients for the ricotta (drained and soaked cashews, water, apple cider vinegar, nutritional yeast, garlic, onions powder, salt, and pepper). Pulse until smooth.

Vegan Bechamel

  1. Melt vegan butter over low heat in a medium saucepan.
  2. Once the butter is completely melted, add the flour. Increase the heat to medium. Whisk together the butter and flour, and allow to cook for about 2 minutes to cut the raw flour flavor.
  3. Add the soymilk all at once. Whisk together continuously. When it comes to a simmer it will thicken. It has reached the right consistency once it coats a wooden spoon. Season with nutmeg, salt, and pepper. Whisk until incorporated.

Layering the Lasagna

  1. Preheat oven to 425 F.
  2. Thinly slice zucchini lengthwise using a mandolin or knide. A mandolin will give you more even slices.
  3. Pour 1 cup of tomato sauce on the bottom of your baking disk. Use a 9x13, or 11x11.
  4. Layer with 4 lasagna sheets, spread another cup of cause over sheets.
  5. Top with one layer of ricotta, a layer of bechemal, once layer of sliced zucchini, one layer of fresh spinach, and another layer of sauce.
  6. Repeat the layering process 2 more times. Top with one layer of lasanga noodles and any left over tomato sauce, and a dollop of bechemal over the top (don't smooth out the top, so that the tomato sauce and bechamel don't mix together. Do it over the top as evenly as possible).
  7. Cover with foil, and bake for 40 minutes. Top with chopped, fresh parsley when the lasagna comes out of the oven, and serve.
